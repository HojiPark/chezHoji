My team leader shared this (for no reason, maybe). I totally agreed with what Alexander said — if we want to be outstanding, we have to do something extraordinary, not like others. I think some people are naturally talented at giving a shit about every piece of work they're responsible for — they might give their best behind the scenes. 

Unfortunately, I can't do that. I tried too, but it always ended in self-criticism. After a few years of struggling, I realized my passion was never driven by responsibility or the goal of being extraordinary — it was the pure joy of making something helpful or joyful for others. 

For me, finding that thing is what matters. Not giving a shit about my work in general, but finding something I can genuinely give a shit about.
